<SubHeading>
	Why I wanted to start over
</SubHeading>

<p>My old Vault was great; I had a lot of content, but I recognized two things I felt I could do better.</p>
<p class="mt-2">Firstly, I had massively gotten stuck in the <InlineLink href="https://zettelkasten.de/posts/collectors-fallacy/">collectors fallacy</InlineLink>.
And I tried to adopt a structure from someone else but needed to understand that system better.</p>

<SubHeading>
	What could I do better
</SubHeading>

<p>Number one and two for me were to restart and build up a system I knew would suit myself and my daily life.</p>
<p class="my-1">This structure I started with could have been better, just that there are better ways to learn how to use other systems.</p>
<p>It's better to start with the bare basics and gradually add what I want to add.</p>

<p class="mt-4">Remember Gall's law:</p>

<Callout emoji="ü¶â" text="All complex systems that work evolved from simpler systems that worked. [And conversely...] A complex system designed from scratch never works and needs to be patched up to make it work." />

<p>I started with a complex system and never used it to my advantage. That is part of why I got stuck in the collector's fallacy.</p>

<SubHeading>
 Daily notes
</SubHeading>

<p>I use my daily notes a lot!</p>
<p class="mt-2">One of the first things I did in my new Vault was to set up a template for my daily notes.</p>

<p class="my-2">My daily note is my funnel to my knowledge database.</p>
<p>I capture almost everything in my daily note. From the daily note, the content will either end up in the slip-box (more on that later), the reference folder, or I will not 'archive it'.</p>

<p class="mt-3">I use raycast on my MacBook pro as a launcher, and some genius made an Obsidian daily note shortcut.</p>
<p class="mt-3">It's super easy to press Command + Option + T, and my daily notes open.</p>

<p class="mt-3">Obsidian has a mobile app, which is perfect for quick capture when you have made some upgrades to it. I sync my Vault to my iCloud (there are more ways to sync your Vault.), so I have the same Vault in my iPhone as in my MacBook.</p>
<p class="mt-1">I used the button plugin to add a custom button to my daily note.</p>

<p class="mt-3">These two methods of quick capture have lowered the "barrier of entry" for me to dump everything into my Vault.</p>
<p class="mt-1">Since implementing this, I have spent more time adding content to my slip-box and making new connections.</p>

<p class="mt-3">Everything that goes to my daily notes could end in my Vault! Not all of it does. I use the daily notes to think, so sometimes. It's just some nonsense about my current work project.</p>
<p class="mt-1">I added a tag to the things I would like to keep on the daily note. For example, I use the #collection tag to collect stuff.</p>

<p>In my weekly notes, I add all of my tasks. These are the tasks I want to complete this week.</p>
<p class="mt-1">Most often, these are long-term-minded tasks that I try to do on most weeks (training, mobility, reading, etc.) combined with more ad-hoc style tasks that I add (pick up this, order haircut, clean apartment, etc.).</p>

<p class="mt-3">I recently added more personal tasks to the bottom of my weekly note. I dearly want to complete these tasks sometime during the week, but to be honest, I've not been good enough at following through with some of them.</p>

Slip-box
</SubHeading>

<p class="mb-2">I put all the notes that I want to 'own' into the slip-box</p>

<p class="my-1">I got the slip-box 'concept' from the book 'How to take smart notes.'</p>
<p class="my-1">I'm not going into many details here because there is no correct way to do this. I like how I structure my slip-box, but there are better ways to do it.</p>
<p>The topic notes work like an entry point to a particular topic. Here below is a list of my current topic notes.</p>

<Heading>
	Conclusion
</Heading>

<p>So, this was quite long üò¨</p>

<p class="mt-2">It may seem like I spend all my days in Obsidian, but that is not the reality.
The more I use my system, the more prolific I become.
Some days I add more. For others, I don't add anything. I don't want to add stuff that I don't need.</p>

<p class="mt-2">If you are interested in using Obsidian, please start small. I slowly integrated and tried everything I use in my Vault before I added it to my workflow. I'm also conscious and willing to remove parts of my system if unnecessary.</p>
<p class="mt-2">But note that this came on the back of my old Vault not working well with me, so I'm deliberately being patient with my system.</p>

<Callout emoji="üî¶" text="The ultimate goal of any productivity system is to have a place where you put tasks and reminders safely so they won't be forgotten and done in time.
 The core reason is that that's the best way to close off open loops, which are nagging sense you forgot something or the random task that will pop up at night before bed.
 Those loops will constantly suck mental energy until you address them, and your brain will spend energy trying to remind you, often at the wrong time and space."/>

<InlineLink href="https://shawnblanc.net/">Shawn Blanc</InlineLink> noted that "there is no one-size fits all system for your tasks, time, and goals. A good system should include these elements:"

<ul class="my-4">
	<li>- Allows you to clarify your priorities</li>
	<li>- It's something you understand and enjoy using</li>
	<li>- It allows you to focus on the _right_ things at the right time</li>
	<li>- Efficiency: you're able to do more in less times (instead of taking a long time to do very little)</li>
	<li>- Anxiety and stress are reduced (not exasperated)</li>
	<li>- You know if you hit the mark</li>
	<li>- It's repeatable and sustainable.</li>
</ul>

<p class="font-semibold">Keep these points in mind when you start building your system üßê</p>
